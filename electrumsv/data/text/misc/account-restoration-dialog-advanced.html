Account Restoration - Advanced options

Provisional in-wallet help

The options shown in this dialog window will depend on the type of account the restoration is being performed for.

Deterministic accounts

An account is classified as "deterministic" if it has the ability to generate new keys on demand. Accounts which are not deterministic, are generally only those with directly imported public keys, private keys and addresses.

Detecting when to stop searching

Certain types of accounts have the ability to create new keys on demand. If the wallet software is restoring an account looking for transactions that use the keys in that account, it can look at known approaches to creating a sequence of keys and keep looking at those sequences until it stops finding used keys along them. The way it works out if there are no more keys, is if it reaches a point in the sequence of keys where there are a given number of unused keys in a row. The technical term for this sequence of unused keys, is the "gap limit."

This section allows the gap limit to be set for the different key sequences. Changing the gap limit is not recommended unless it is known that something has gone wrong in the past with the key sequences causing unexpected gaps to occur. Servers can rate limit the free access they give to the services the restorer uses, and in the future may charge for more than a small amount of free daily usage.

Change

When a user is paying someone else, the coins from their account that they pay with rarely add up to exactly the amount they are paying. The left over balance is sent back to the account the funds come from. This change from the payment is placed in payment destinations using keys from a specific sequence of keys, the change keys. This is the same concept as the change that is made when physical notes and coins are used for payment.

Received funds

When a user is planning on receiving funds, they select a payment destination it should go to and give it to the payer. These payment destinations are created including keys from a specific sequence of keys, the receiving keys.
